---
name: US Regulatory Landscape for Data Center Cooling
description: Key regulatory factors by state that affect cooling technology decisions in US data centers
type: reference
---

## Virginia (us-va) — Complexity: 58/100
- **Sales tax exemption** (§58.1-609.3): Covers cooling equipment including chillers, CDUs, cooling towers, pumps, piping. Critical selling point for liquid cooling CapEx. Valid through 2035 but under 2026 legislative review.
- **VCEA**: 100% clean electricity by 2045 via Dominion Energy
- No mandatory energy efficiency code for commercial buildings (data centers)
- Water withdrawal permits from DEQ if evaporative cooling exceeds thresholds

## Texas (us-tx) — Complexity: 44/100
- No statewide commercial energy code — minimal regulatory pressure on cooling choices
- **TCEQ water reporting** (Feb 2026): New mandatory survey of data center water use by cooling type — evaporative cooling faces scrutiny
- ERCOT interconnection review for loads over 75 MW
- RICE NESHAP applies to backup generators
- Most permissive state for new data center development

## California (us-ca) — Complexity: 82/100
- **Title 24 Part 6 2025 Standards** (eff. Jan 1 2026): 100% economizer cooling required; fan power limits; containment mandates. Strongest mandatory cooling efficiency code in the US.
- **SB 57**: CPUC study of data center electricity rates due Jan 2027 — potential for tiered rates
- **CARB/AQMD**: Strict generator air permits; 200 hr/yr non-emergency limit; non-attainment zones
- **CEQA**: Environmental review for new construction requires analysis of water use and cooling type
- AB 93 (water use pre-reporting) was vetoed Oct 2025 but signals future direction

## Oregon (us-or) — Complexity: 55/100
- **POWER Act (HB 3546, June 2025)**: New rate class for >20 MW users including data centers — costs going up
- OECC commercial energy code requires economizer controls
- Prior appropriation water rights — evaporative cooling may face permit delays
- Enterprise Zone property tax exemptions attract investment; cooling equipment qualifies

## Illinois (us-il) — Complexity: 65/100
- **Data Center Investment Tax Exemption** (20 ILCS 605/605-1025): Sales tax exemption up to 20 years on cooling equipment. SUSPENDED for new applicants July 1, 2026 for 2 years.
- **POWER Act (proposed 2026)**: Would mandate efficient cooling, water reporting, and clean energy plans
- Chicago metro is non-attainment area — stricter AQMD permitting for generators
- Large volume water withdrawal (>100k gal/day) requires registration under Water Use Act

## Federal (all zones)
- **RICE NESHAP (40 CFR Part 63 Subpart ZZZZ)**: Backup generators limited to 100 hr/yr non-emergency. Annual testing required.
- **EPA ENERGY STAR for Data Centers**: Score 75+ to certify; no direct PUE threshold but PUE improvement drives score
- **DOE FEMP Best Practices Guide** (July 2024 revision): Voluntary guidance; used in federal procurement
- **Clean Air Act Title V**: Major source permits for high-emission facilities

**How to apply:** When advising on state selection for new liquid cooling deployments: TX is easiest (no code), CA is hardest (Title 24 + CEQA). VA tax exemption makes it attractive financially. IL exemption expiring for new entrants as of mid-2026.
