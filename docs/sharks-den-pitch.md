The world is building data centers at an unprecedented rate — over $700 billion in capital expenditure this year alone. Every one of these facilities generates massive amounts of heat. Traditional air cooling can't keep up. NVIDIA's latest Blackwell GPUs pull 1,200 watts each and *require* liquid cooling. There is no air-cooled option.

The data center cooling market is projected to hit $7 billion by 2029. And right now, most operators are scrambling to figure out how to cool their facilities efficiently.

## THE SOLUTION — Solar Steam (20 seconds)

Solar Steam is a liquid cooling and emerging immersion cooling company. We help data center operators reduce their energy consumption for cooling by up to 90%, cut water usage to near zero, and achieve Power Usage Effectiveness ratings as low as 1.04 — compared to the industry average of 1.56.

Our target market is facilities under 10 megawatts — the colocation providers, the edge deployments, the AI factories being built right now — where our technology is the right size and the right price.
